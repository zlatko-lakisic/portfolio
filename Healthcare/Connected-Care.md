# Connected Care & Private Networks

[← Healthcare Architecture](./README.md) · [← Main Portfolio](../index.md)

## Business problem

Health systems and medical-device OEMs need reliable, secure connectivity beyond consumer-grade WiFi — for connected devices, campus-wide clinical workflows, and partner-integrated care delivery. Verizon's private network portfolio required an architect who could translate clinical and operational requirements into deployable connectivity solutions.

## Constraints

- **Regulated environments** — healthcare-grade reliability expectations and vendor security reviews
- **Multi-stakeholder sales** — clinical leadership, IT, facilities, and OEM engineering teams
- **Proof before scale** — conference demonstrations and pilot deployments before enterprise rollout
- **OEM partnership model** — connectivity as an integrated element, not a standalone commodity

## Architecture

- **Private cellular networks** — campus and facility coverage for mission-critical device and workflow connectivity
- **OEM co-innovation** — joint roadmaps from conference demo through co-innovation agreement to production deployment
- **Integrated solution positioning** — pairing Verizon connectivity with complementary COTS platforms
- **Vertical playbooks** — repeatable discovery and architecture patterns for health-system accounts

## Tradeoffs

- **Depth vs breadth** — focused on high-value health-system and OEM wins rather than generic connectivity bids
- **Demo investment** — significant pre-sales engineering to prove value before contract signature
- **Partner dependency** — success tied to OEM platform adoption timelines alongside network deployment

## Outcome

- **Baxter Healthcare** — instrumental in securing Verizon's first private network sale with Baxter; championed conference demonstration that clarified technology value ([Sam Aria](../Recommendations/sam-aria.md))
- **Broader connected-hospital wins** — drove multiple healthcare organizations toward cutting-edge wireless for connected care
- **Healthcare OEM pipeline** — co-innovation agreement, six-figure network deployment, and long-term cellular adoption roadmap from conference through production
- **Multi-million-dollar engagements** — contributed to private network opportunities at leading U.S. health systems
